Fatal Road Transport Crashes
in Australia

A fatal road transport crash recorded in the Australian Road Deaths Database (ARDD) is a crash accident involving road transportations and causes at least one death.

The period studied is between 01 January 2015 to 31 August 2021.

This visualisation aims to explore the trends and patterns of fatal road transport crashes in Australia.

Fatal Crashes vs Average Deaths per Fatal Crash by SA4

The regression line in the bubble plot shows a slight positive correlation between the number of fatal crashes and the average number of deaths per fatal crash.

Generally, as the number of fatal crashes increases, more opportunities for higher number of deaths per fatal crash arise which consequently increases the average number of deaths per fatal crash.

However, the average number of deaths per fatal crash is lesser for more populated SA4s. Major cities with higher population tend to have better infrastructure safety features, higher level of safety enforcements and faster post-crash emergency responses. This indicates that there is a greater risk in regional and remote areas.
