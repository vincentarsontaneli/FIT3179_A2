Fatal Road Transport Crashes
in Australia

A fatal road transport crash recorded in the Australian Road Deaths Database (ARDD) is a reported crash accident involving at least one road vehicle in a road-related area and causes at least one death.

A Statistical Area Level 4 (SA4) is the largest sub-state region in the main structure of Australia.

The period studied is between 01 January 2015 to 31 August 2021.

This visualisation aims to explore the trends and patterns of fatal road transport crashes in Australia.

Fatal Crashes per 100,000 population by SA4 from 2015 to 2020

From 2015 to 2020, New South Wales (NSW) consistently has the highest total number of fatal crashes per 100,000 population among the states, having a minimum difference of 40 with the second highest state each year, except in 2020 when NSW experienced a large reduction of 27.9% from the previous year. On the other hand, Queensland (Qld) experienced a large increase of 21.2% from 2019 to 2020 with a peak value of 110.44.

Note: 2021 was omitted given that the available data only covers up to August 2021, which makes the crash count for 2021 considerably lower than other years.

Fatal Crashes vs Average Deaths per Fatal Crash by SA4

The regression line in the bubble plot shows a slight positive correlation between the number of fatal crashes and the average number of deaths per fatal crash.

SA4s in Qld have alarmingly high death-to-crash ratios compared to SA4s in other states. Townsville and Cairns have an an average of 1.20 and 1.19 deaths per fatal crash with almost 60 fatal crashes each.

However, the average number of deaths per fatal crash is lesser for more populated SA4s. Victoria (Vic) and NSW have SA4s with large populations and relatively low average deaths such as Melbourne - South East. Major cities tend to have more safety enforcements and faster post-crash emergency responses.



















Note: the y-axis starts from 1.00 because each fatal road transport crash results at least one death, which means the minimum death-to-crash ratio is 1.00.
